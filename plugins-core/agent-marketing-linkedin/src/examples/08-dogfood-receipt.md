We used lope to launch lope.

This is not a metaphor. Every piece of the lope launch campaign was negotiated through lope itself.

The marketing plan: lope negotiate --domain business "lope public launch campaign". Ensemble validators flagged that the first draft conflated launch-week metrics with long-term retention metrics. Draft revised. Passed at round two.

The blog posts: drafted by a primary CLI, reviewed by two others playing senior editors. Round one caught an overclaim about "production-ready" status. Revised to "v0.3.0, first public release, use at your own rate of learning." Passed.

The LinkedIn sequence: each post compressed in caveman mode to keep validator prompts tight. Ten posts, two rounds of ensemble review, one NEEDS_FIX on post seven for buried lede. Rewritten. Passed.

The origin post: audited by a validator from a different model family specifically to avoid self-congratulation loops. It caught three overclaims. Three.

If lope worked on itself first, that's the proof. If the launch flops, I know exactly which phase failed and why, because I have the scorecard.
